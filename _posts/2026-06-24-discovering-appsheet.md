Semester 1 2026 exams done and dusted! Winter break is officially on and its my pleasure to report I am not spending the break in chilly Brisbane.

Instead I am spending the next 3 weeks in Pisa. (Help, its currently 40 degrees 😅)

But that doesn't mean work stops! With the bonus spare time I am keen to get stuck into a project that directly relates to my Business Information System Major. This project is to build out a bespoke App for Newish Communications Inc.

I am building it using AppSheet, a Google product that lets you develop full applications without writing any code. Since Newish runs entirely on Google Workspace it slots in perfectly with our existing setup. This was an especially amazing find as it is exactly the solution I have been looking for!

To begin with, I am going to build out modules commonly found in a Human Resources Information System (HRIS). As the Director of Human Resources and Operations this was the highest priority, with modules Timesheeting, Leave management and an Analytics Dashboard being priority.

Currently I have built out the database and a few views, including a form for submitting leave requests, a table for viewing leave requests and a dashboard for my team and Executives to view analytics.

[Screenshot of current views]

AppSheet lets you define a database, build out views like forms, tables and dashboards, set user permissions and deploy a working app on both mobile and desktop. All through a visual editor, no code required.

I am also going to use this break to start the Google Skills AppSheet Developer Course. Its just over 30 hours in total and so the timing feels right, plus it felt like a no brainer since this will be a bespoke HRIS built around Newish's unique structure. Our membership is made up entirely of students, meaning we have a relatively high turnover with a maximum retention of around 4 years, so having a system purpose built for us makes a lot more sense than an off the shelf solution.

Which means we will end up with a bespoke HRIS built specifically for Newish, that anyone on the team can understand and maintain down the line.
